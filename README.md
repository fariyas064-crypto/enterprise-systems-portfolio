Research and conversations with students showed that many felt confused or overwhelmed during registration, especially first-year, international, transfer, and nontraditional-path students. Students also reported relying on friends or group chats instead of official resources, while advisors were often difficult to access during peak registration periods.

## System Story
The existing registration experience creates unnecessary cognitive load by forcing students to switch between advising documents, degree audits, and registration tools. It also makes it difficult for students to understand prerequisites, course order, and conflicts in their schedule.

This project proposes a better website and registration experience that organizes information more clearly and supports students through the full registration process. The larger system vision includes features such as integrated planning, clearer prerequisite visibility, conflict warnings, schedule optimization, and seamless connection to related academic systems.

## Proposed Solution
The redesigned system focuses on:
- clearer navigation through registration steps
- integrated planning support
- better visibility of prerequisites and course sequencing
- early warnings for schedule conflicts
- a more unified and student-centered registration flow

In the long-term vision, the system could expand into an AutoEnroll AI platform that helps students generate conflict-free schedules, detect issues in real time, and connect registration with calendars, degree progress, and academic planning tools.
